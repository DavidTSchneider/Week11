PhoneGap: Definition

PhoneGap is an application container technology that allows one to create natively-installed applications for mobile devices using HTML, CSS, and Javascript.

Why use PhoneGap?

PhoneGap, whether it is installed on your home computer or you use its online counterpart PhoneGap Build, is a quick, direct means of creating an app for those individuals who are not trained in the programming languages of the various devices such as Java and Objective C.

Initial Setup

After signing up for free on GitHub.com, download the program on your home computer. This local installation will allow you to set up storage folders known as repositories where you will place the files that will, ultimately, be converted into an application. Make sure you save your HTML page as index.html. This document must also be accompanied by all dependent files such as CSS, Javascript, and Images.

Once the files are loaded into the repository, proceed to click "commit changes" and "publish" to complete the GitHub preparation.


Converting to an App

Now in PhoneGap, click "New App" and "Open Source". You will then click on the dropdown list menu, to select your chosen repository for its conversion. Click "Ready to Build" to initiate the metamorphosis. As your documents are being changed, you will see icons representing the various mobile brandnames become highlighted. This represents the new applications' preparedness on those particular platforms.

Installation on your Device

Now that the conversion process in complete, click on "Public Page" to proceed to the download window. This page is represented by a QR Code at the bottom of the list of mobile brands.

Hold your mobile device up to this QR code and scan it. This will initiate the downloading of the proper app for your particular device. Once copied over to your mobile platform, click install to enjoy your new application.
